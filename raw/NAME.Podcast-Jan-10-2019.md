**Title:** Judgment as a Filter for Reality  
**Date:** January 10, 2019  
**Speaker:** Allon Schaham  
**Setting:** Early morning post-walk reflection, cold winter day  

---

**Transcript:**

Good morning, everybody. It is January 10th, 2019, and today is gonna be an absolutely incredible day. I can feel it. Without further ado, today’s *Daily Stoic* passage comes from Epictetus:

> “The essence of good is a certain kind of reasoned choice, just as the essence of evil is another kind. What about externals then? They are only the raw material for our reasoned choice, which finds its own good or evil in working with them…” — Epictetus, Discourses 1.29.1–3

The commentary focuses on clarity and steadiness. The Stoics believed we don’t need silence or solitude to find peace — we need sound judgment. The world is chaos. Our minds can be filters. When our judgments are clean, our experience becomes ordered. When they’re warped, everything gets distorted.

What I get from this is: we are the lens. Life doesn’t get to define us. We define how we receive life.

I’ve started a new job recently, and it’s been surprisingly good. Not because the job is easy or perfect — but because my *attitude* is different. My judgment is better. I’m not internalizing negativity. I’m not bottling things up. I’m expressing myself clearly and moving forward without resentment.

A younger version of me would have festered. Gotten bitter. Shut down. But now, I use the filter. I ask: is this worth internalizing? Does this belong inside my mind?

The morning was cold again. I wore three layers, long johns, and a snowboard jacket. Still could only do a mile and a half. But I’m proud of showing up. Of pushing through. Of facing discomfort without quitting.

So — today, ask yourself: are you using your judgment as a filter? Or are you letting the world seep in unfiltered?

Stay focused. Stay clear. Stay grounded.

Have a wonderful day.
