**Forever Flowers – Creative Hierarchy (Inspired by Virgil Abloh)**

**Governed by:** Brand Steward

---

**1. Conceptual Narrative > Product Form**
We do not sell flowers. We freeze moments. Each bouquet begins as a feeling, a memory, or a ritual. The preserved florals are a medium for storytelling—not decoration.

**2. Visual Symbolism > Decorative Design**
Bouquet structure, scale, color, and spacing are deliberate. They evoke emotion, not just beauty. Nothing is ornamental unless it serves the story.

**3. Restraint = Luxury**
Minimalism is our flex. We use whitespace, limited copy, and grid discipline to elevate perception. Every design choice must justify itself. Clarity is the highest form of confidence.

**4. Cultural Referencing > Originality Theater**
We borrow from the domestic Israeli home, from Japanese order, from European restraint. We remix, reframe, and reposition. The twist *is* the point.

**5. Typography = Emotion**
Words are visual. Headlines carry tone before meaning. Spacing, weight, and placement communicate status. “Eternal Love” is not just text—it’s design.

**6. Archive = Sacred**
What’s no longer for sale becomes legend. Our Past Seasons are not dead inventory. They are our brand’s evolving retrospective—untouchable, inspirational, and eternal.

---

**Usage:**
This creative hierarchy applies to all visual design, product layout, copy tone, and seasonal presentation. It guides Forever Flowers’ design team, developers, copywriters, and external collaborators.
