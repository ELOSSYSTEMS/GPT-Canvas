## 1. Purpose
Define *why* the Forever Flowers website behaves the way it does, so the system stays coherent under growth, new contributors, and optimization pressure.

This is not brand copy. This is decision doctrine.

---

## 2. Market Doctrine (Israeli Luxury)

### 2.1 Core Observations
Israeli luxury buyers:
- Read signals fast and punish uncertainty.
- Prefer competence over warmth at first contact.
- Distrust marketing density.
- Interpret optionality as indecision.
- Prefer “quiet confidence” over “explained value.”

### 2.2 What Signals Luxury (Behavioral)
Luxury is conveyed by:
- **Restraint** (what you do not say)
- **Defaults** (what you assume is correct)
- **Precision** (facts without drama)
- **Appropriateness** (fit to space, not fit to ego)
- **Operational competence** (especially B2B)

### 2.3 What Breaks Luxury
Luxury collapses when the site exhibits:
- Persuasion tone
- Excess copy density
- Decorative storytelling
- Feature lists without hierarchy
- Over-qualification (“to be honest”, “we try”, “maybe”)
- Too many choices too early
- Emotional exposure (“mine”, “who I am”, “my story” as primary)

## 3. The Tier Philosophy

### 3.1 The Tier Model
The site enforces a ladder of commitment:

- **TRY** = low commitment, immediate fit, minimal responsibility
- **DECORATE** = medium commitment, statement and presence
- **CUSTOMIZE** = high commitment, service relationship and shared responsibility

### 3.2 Non-Negotiable Rule
**Tiers are expressed through pages, not through personalization logic.**

This means:
- No “customer tier” determines hidden blocks on the same URL.
- No conditional rendering based on guessed intent.
- No personalization that changes meaning of a page.

Users climb by navigation and desire, not by gating.

### 3.3 Tier Success Criteria
A tier is correct when it:
- Lowers perceived effort
- Increases certainty
- Preserves dignity
- Makes the next step obvious without pressure

## 4. The Site’s Psychological Contract

### 4.1 What We Promise Implicitly
The site promises:
- You will not be pressured.
- You will not be embarrassed.
- You will not be asked to “be creative.”
- You will not be left to interpret scale.
- You will not be punished for choosing small.

### 4.2 What We Refuse Implicitly
The site refuses:
- Noise
- Negotiation tone
- Sales games
- False intimacy

---

## 5. Restraint Doctrine

### 5.1 The Default Bias
When uncertain, remove rather than add.

### 5.2 The “One-Line” Discipline
If a concept cannot be stated in one sentence:
- It belongs lower on the page, or
- It belongs on another page, or
- It does not belong.

### 5.3 Absence Is a Feature
If a block is absent, it is absent because its presence would:
- Increase effort
- Invite comparison shopping
- Trigger skepticism
- Create emotional exposure
- Dilute authority

---

## 6. Language & Tone Doctrine

### 6.1 Tone Requirements
All public-facing UX text should be:
- Descriptive
- Calm
- Precise
- Non-performative
- Low-emotion

### 6.2 Prohibited Language Patterns
Avoid:
- Urgency (limited time, last chance)
- Validation (you deserve, treat yourself)
- Over-personal identity talk
- Budget framing (affordable, worth the money)

### 6.3 Typography of Meaning (Conceptual)
Even before visual design:
- Short lines = authority
- White space = confidence
- Hierarchy = competence

## 7. Page Behavior Rules (Derived)

### 7.1 Homepage
- Routes. Does not sell.
- Single sentence hero.
- Presents the ladder without instruction.

### 7.2 PDPs
- Facts first.
- Scale and care are not optional.
- No service simulation.

### 7.3 Portfolio
- Proof without narration.
- Images lead; text stays minimal.

### 7.4 B2B
- Operations language.
- Reliability and lifecycle.
- No décor fantasy.

## 8. Canonical Decision Tests
Use these tests to approve or reject any change.

### Test A: Effort
Does this reduce perceived effort? If no, reject.

### Test B: Certainty
Does this increase certainty quickly? If no, move it down or remove.

### Test C: Dignity
Does this avoid shame/pressure/over-sharing? If no, reject.

### Test D: Tier Integrity
Does this introduce CUSTOMIZE language into TRY? If yes, reject.

### Test E: Luxury Signal
Does this add noise, icons, arrows, instruction? If yes, reject.
