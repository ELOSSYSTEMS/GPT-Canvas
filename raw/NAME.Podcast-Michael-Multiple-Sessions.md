**Podcast Transcript Compilation – Conversations with Michael**

---

**Recording Span:** Multiple sessions (2018–2019)
**Participants:** Allon Schaham & Michael
**Context:** Late-night philosophical dialogues, introspective analysis, and cultural breakdowns

---

**Session 1 – [Untitled Session, Late 2018]**

Allon: We never get this deep in person.

Michael: That’s because we never shut up long enough to listen.

Allon: You think the podcast format makes us better versions of ourselves?

Michael: Nah. I think it just makes us less distracted.

Allon: I think it makes me more aware of how I sound. And how little time I spend in silence.

Michael: That’s probably the most New York thing you’ve ever said.

---

**Session 2 – [Late 2018, “Digital Boundaries” Segment]**

Allon: If you had to go one year without a smartphone or a laptop, what would you do?

Michael: That’s easy. I’d write. Pen and paper. Rediscover slowness.

Allon: You always say that, but you’re still on your phone every day.

Michael: Which is why I need to stop. It’s why we all say it. Because we know we can’t.

Allon: Is that addiction or conditioning?

Michael: Same thing.

[They segue into a discussion about tech minimalism, digital fasting, and the paradox of mindful consumption.]

---

**Session 3 – [“Memory, Identity, and Regret” Episode]**

Allon: Do you remember what you wanted to be when you were ten?

Michael: Honestly? I think I wanted to disappear.

Allon: I wanted to be seen. Funny how that works.

Michael: And now?

Allon: Now I want to be remembered. But for the right reasons.

Michael: What does that even mean?

Allon: That I left something clean behind. Something that didn’t rot.

[Conversation explores legacy, journal-writing, mortality, and the role of truth in personal narrative.]

---

**Session 4 – [Philosophical Freeform, 2019]**

Michael: What’s the point of podcasts?

Allon: To remember what we sounded like before we had kids, jobs, and mortgages.

Michael: You think this is preservation?

Allon: No. I think it’s a mirror.

Michael: You think everyone wants to hear themselves talk.

Allon: No. I think they want to feel themselves think.

[Themes: ego, introspection, the role of voice in constructing memory.]
