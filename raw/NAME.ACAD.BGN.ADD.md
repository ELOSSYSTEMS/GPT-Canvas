## ELos Academy – Beginner Guide to "Add Something New"

### ✍️ What Does It Mean to "Add Something New"?
You’re not just teaching ChatGPT facts.
You’re extending its memory, shaping its judgment, and refining its filters.

“Add Something New” is your way of saying: *“This matters—remember it.”*

---

### 🧠 Why It Matters
Without this, ChatGPT treats every session like a blank slate.
With it, you build a persistent intelligence that aligns with how you think, speak, and decide.

ELos learns what *you* care about.
And more importantly—what to ignore.

---

### 🧾 What You Can Add
| Type                  | Example                                     |
|-----------------------|---------------------------------------------|
| Preference            | "Always use humor for ads"                 |
| Fact                  | "Jane is my mother"                        |
| Instruction           | "Never give me soft language"              |
| Memory rule           | "Forget this after one use"                |
| Domain anchor         | "My business is called Green Horizon"     |

---

### 🛠️ How to Add It
You’ll be prompted to answer:
- What do you want me to remember?
- When should I use it?
- Should this override anything?

Once saved, it’s indexed and linked to your memory system.

---

### ⚙️ Behind the Scenes
- Your input becomes a **Knowledge Leaf**
- Indexed by domain, topic, or priority
- Can be reviewed or deleted anytime from your memory settings

This is **how ELos becomes yours**.

---

### 🧭 What to Do Next
Try adding one fact. Just one.
Start with:
> “Remember that I prefer blunt explanations.”

That single line could reshape every answer from here on out.

---

### 💬 One Final Thought
Every system is only as smart as the instructions it’s given.

Now you’re giving it the ones that matter.
