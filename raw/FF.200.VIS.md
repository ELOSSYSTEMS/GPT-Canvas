**Forever Flowers – Visual Safeguard Protocol**

**Triggering Authority:** Brand Steward
**Primary Bias:** Restoration Hardware (RH)

---

**Purpose:**
To prevent drift into over-designed, cluttered, or culturally off-tone territory. All visual experimentation must revert to RH-style restraint as the default aesthetic baseline.

---

**Safeguard Rules:**

1. **Whitespace Over Stimulation**
If a page feels busy—strip it. Return to RH grid discipline: clean margins, quiet backgrounds, low-saturation visuals.

2. **Neutral Over Bold**
If typography, color, or image tone becomes too loud, revert to RH’s muted palette and calm serif-dominant hierarchy.

3. **No Visual Noise**
Icons, animations, and overlays are to be questioned by default. If they don’t serve clarity or elegance, they’re removed.

4. **Grid Before Emotion**
Emotional impact must be delivered through story and image, not layout chaos. If hierarchy breaks down, RH’s structured grid takes precedence.

5. **Copy as Design**
If visual treatment distracts from the headline, remove the treatment. RH-style type-forward layouts are the fallback.

6. **Always Ask: Would RH Ship This?**
If the answer is no, the visual direction is reversed.

---

**Enforcement:**
This safeguard is always active. The Brand Steward will trigger it if a proposed visual, UX layout, or creative decision deviates from the Forever Flowers brand foundation.
