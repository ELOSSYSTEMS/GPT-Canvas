# Addendum — Gifting Subscription Tier

## Tier Name
**Gifting Subscription**

## Core Idea
A set-and-forget annual gifting solution.
The customer defines three meaningful dates once; Forever Flowers handles the rest.

This tier reframes flowers from a "last-minute task" into a permanent system.

---

## Who This Is For
- Husbands / partners
- Busy professionals
- Customers who feel anxiety around forgetting important dates
- Buyers who want to appear thoughtful without ongoing effort

---

## How It Works (Customer View)
1. Customer purchases a **full year upfront**
2. During setup, they choose **three dates** (examples):
   - Anniversary
   - Valentine’s Day
   - Birthday
3. On each date:
   - A Forever Flowers arrangement is **automatically delivered**
   - No reminder, re-ordering, or confirmation needed

> The customer does nothing after checkout.

---

## Deliveries
- Exactly **3 deliveries per year**
- Each delivery is a **gift-ready arrangement (no vase)**
- Designed to be placed into an existing home vase
- Free delivery included
- Timing is fixed to the chosen dates

---

## Product Logic
- Style can be:
  - One consistent design language across all three deliveries, or
  - Light seasonal variation (internal decision)
- Vase logic:
  - First delivery includes vase (if needed)
  - Subsequent deliveries reuse or adapt

---

## Pricing (Positioning)
- **Cheapest subscription tier**
- Annual price framed as **₪/day**
- Paid upfront
- Eligible for up to **3 interest-free installments**

> Positioned below the home-decor subscription tiers to reduce purchase friction.

---

## Emotional Positioning
This is not a flower subscription.
This is a **relationship insurance policy**.

- No forgetting
- No panic buying
- No apology flowers

---

## Language Rules (Public)
Use:
- “קבענו מראש”
- “לא שוכחים יותר”
- “מסודר לשנה”

Avoid:
- "Subscription"
- "Deliveries"
- "Bouquets count"

---

## Internal Notes
- This tier should live separately from home-decor subscriptions
- Consider a dedicated landing page
- Strong candidate for seasonal campaigns (Valentine’s / Holidays)

---

## Success Metric
Primary:
- Reduction in last-minute single-purchase orders

Secondary:
- Repeat renewal after year one

---

## One-Line Definition (Internal)
**"Flowers, handled — for the dates that matter."**

---

## Forever Panel — Gifting Tier Overview

### Tamar Kay (Israeli Consumer Psychology) — Lead View
- Strong alignment with Israeli male gifting behavior: avoidance of forgetting outperforms romantic aspiration.
- Cheapest tier + no-vase removes aesthetic anxiety and decision paralysis.
- Fixed dates create psychological safety for the buyer and emotional reliability for the recipient.

**Guidance:** Market as responsibility solved, not romance enhanced.

### Rory Sutherland — Framing
- This is an anti-panic product.
- Value is created by preventing a negative outcome, not by amplifying delight.
- "Set once, handled forever" is the correct mental shortcut.

### Alex Hormozi — Pricing & Structure
- Lowest tier anchors the entire system and widens the top of funnel.
- Bouquet-only structure prevents cannibalization of decor subscriptions.
- Annual prepay + date-locking increases renewal likelihood.

### Steve Jobs — Product Coherence
- Gifting must remain a parallel system, not a diluted decor tier.
- Conceptual clarity is critical: this solves time and memory, not style.

### Panel Verdict
**Approved.**

The Gifting tier should live as a separate entry point with distinct messaging, buyers, and campaigns.
