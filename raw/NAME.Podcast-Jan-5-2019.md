Hello and welcome to the OG podcast. This is the first installment. It's January 5th. It's 9:45 p.m. I'm actually getting ready for bed and I figured before I do that I should just release this first podcast. Talk about who I am and what I'm going to be speaking about.

My name is Allon. I live in New York City. I'm 36. Been through a lot in my life — wouldn’t say that I had a difficult life, but definitely had some ups and downs like most of us have. I spent a lot of time away from Israel, a place that I consider my second home — Tel Aviv — for about a decade. And about three weeks ago, I had the privilege of going. And I had a really, really transformative and really, really great time.

It's something that I feel really fortunate to have experienced. And I think that it's turned my life around in a positive way. I spent many, many years in a long, deep hole — very depressed, very sad. And I'm glad to say that I feel like those days are behind me. I'm probably going to stumble along the way. Hopefully, I get to share some of those moments with you. And through that, we’ll grow together.

I'm just trying to keep some positive energy, keep that momentum. Just let you guys know how my days are going.

Before I get into anything crazy, I think I should tell you the main reason why I had this turnaround — in case any one of you guys are in the same position. You know, not feeling so great, not feeling happy about where you are in life.

The first step is: **Be impeccable with your word.** I take that to mean your word is your bond. Do what you say and say what you do. Pretty straightforward.

**Second:** Don’t take anything personally. That was a big one for me — a lot of times I would take things in the wrong light, or take it personally when it really wasn’t about me. It would snowball into something much larger that could’ve just been a small molehill.

**Third:** Don’t make assumptions. You never know the whole story. Even if you do, there could be parts that you don’t know. Try not to assume things — take them at face value. Like Freud said: “Sometimes a cigar is just a cigar.”

**Fourth:** Do your best. It’s one of the only things you can control. Doesn’t cost money. It’s just hard work. If you implement these four things every day, I truly believe you’ll lead a better and more fulfilling life.

These four principles are from a book I read on the flight there and back: *The Four Agreements.* I truly believe that they helped me keep momentum in a positive direction.

**Second thing I wanted to mention:** my sleeping habits. I started sleeping earlier — around 9 or 10 p.m. I realized that between 10 and midnight, I’d just sit on the couch watching TV or doing nothing productive. I cut those hours out and added them to the morning.

Now I go to sleep between 9 and 10 p.m. and wake up between 4 and 5 a.m. When I get up, I drink a glass of water, make my bed, stretch a little, and go for a heavy walk — around 3 to 3.5 miles. Then I shower, and ideally sit down and record a short podcast or gratitude session.

I recently bought *The Daily Stoic.* Every morning I read a little stoic tidbit — a quote and commentary — and try to absorb and apply it that day. I’ll share those in the mornings going forward.

So, we’ll start the day positive and carry that momentum. I hope you give me feedback. And then the next morning, I’ll share how I implemented it.

Start your day right. Make your bed. Drink a tall glass of water. And start strong.

Have a great night, everyone.
