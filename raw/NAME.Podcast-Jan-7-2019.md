**Transcript:**

Good morning everyone. It’s January 7th, 2019. It’s about 7:39 AM, and I just came back from another brisk, early morning walk. Today I did about two and a half miles. It was still a little cold out, so I layered up again, but it’s not as brutal as it’s been.

Let’s jump right into today’s *Daily Stoic* reading. The title is: **Don’t Tell Yourself a Story**

> “This is what you should teach me, how to be like Odysseus—how to love my country, wife, and father, and how, even after suffering shipwreck, he kept sailing on.” —Seneca, Moral Letters, 88.7b–8

The commentary reflects on how easy it is to construct narratives in our heads. Stories about why we’re failing. Why something isn’t fair. Why we can’t be happy. But Stoicism rejects all of that. It says: get to the root of things. Don’t dramatize. Don’t tell yourself a tale. Just see what’s in front of you and act.

What I took away from this: A lot of us build these cinematic, exaggerated explanations for what we’re going through. We lie to ourselves. We create false complexity. It’s our ego protecting itself — crafting reasons and excuses instead of focusing on action.

Odysseus didn’t let the storm define him. He kept going. And so must we.

Today, I’m reminding myself: no stories, no victimhood, no mental excuses. Just the reality of what needs doing. I’ve got my schedule, I’ve got my goals, and I’ve got the ability to choose how I respond. That’s enough.

It’s crazy to say this, but walking first thing in the morning and recording these little segments — they’re already changing my trajectory. There’s something about consistent self-talk — intentional self-talk — that overrides the negativity in your mind. There’s something about hearing your own voice articulate your values and plans that makes them real.

You have a voice inside your head. But most of us never program it. We just let it run wild. I’m trying to take control of mine, one morning at a time.

So for you today: what story are you telling yourself? And what would happen if you just cut the story and got on with it?

That’s it for now. I’ll be back tomorrow. I hope you walk, stretch, drink some water, and move forward with intention. Have a great one.
