**Transcript:**

Good morning everybody. It is January 9th, 2019. I didn’t have such a wonderful morning. I skipped my morning walk. I woke up groggy, and I'm not exactly sure why — I went to sleep at the right time. So, I figured I’d just read you the complete passage from *The Daily Stoic* today, instead of improvising.

The title is: **What We Control and What We Don’t**

> “Some things are in our control while others are not. We control our opinion, choice, desire, aversion… We don’t control our body, property, reputation, position… Even more, the things in our control are by nature free, unhindered… while those not in our control are weak, slavish, can be hindered.” — Epictetus, Enchiridion 1.1–2

The commentary: You won’t control the external events that happen. That might be scary. But what balances it is that you do control your opinion about them. You decide what’s good, what’s bad. What’s worth your energy. The outside world gives you raw material — your judgment is the filter.

This morning, that hit me hard. I didn’t wake up on time. I didn’t go for my walk. But I’m not going to internalize that as failure. I’m not going to spiral. What I can do is redirect. So I’m doing this podcast anyway. I’m using my voice. I’m showing up, even if I didn’t do everything perfectly.

Later today, I’ll walk instead. I’ll make that trade. And tomorrow I’ll try again to wake up at 4 or 5 AM.

Stoicism says: don’t get upset at what’s outside your control. Reinforce what you *can* do. That’s what I’m trying to do.

So — get up, reset, and push on. Control what’s yours. Have a great day, everyone.
