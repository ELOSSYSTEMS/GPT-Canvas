### Purpose
This canvas exists to chronicle, investigate, and unpack the ideological, political, and cultural roots of Zionism and Israeli statehood. It serves as a recursive research kernel dedicated to understanding:

- The historical evolution of Zionist thought from pre-Herzlian streams to political Zionism
- The ideological splits (Labor, Revisionist, Religious, Post-Zionist)
- The impact of colonialism, nationalism, religion, diaspora, and trauma on Zionist identity formation
- The founding myths and counter-myths of Israeli history
- The interaction between Israeli state-building and Jewish global identity

This is not a passive archive — it's a cognitive excavation site.

---

### Structure
1. **Core Inquiry Threads**
   - What is Zionism? Who defined it, and how has it mutated?
   - Is Zionism a colonial project, a liberation movement, or both?
   - What ideologies were suppressed in the process of state formation?
   - How does the memory of the Shoah shape Israeli identity and foreign policy?
   - What are the blind spots in the mainstream Israeli narrative?

2. **Primary Source Pools** (To be expanded)
   - Writings by Herzl, Jabotinsky, Ahad Ha’am, Ben-Gurion, Buber
   - Brit Shalom documents, Revisionist Zionism texts
   - UN Partition Plan, Balfour Declaration, Peel Commission
   - Testimonies from Mizrahi, Palestinian, ultra-Orthodox, and anti-Zionist Jewish voices

3. **Analysis Modules**
   - Timeline Reconstruction
   - Ideology Dissection
   - Founding Myth Critique
   - Comparative Nationalism: Zionism vs. other liberation movements

4. **Recursive Questions**
   - What are we not allowed to question?
   - What narratives are state-sanctioned, and which are silenced?
   - Who benefits from each version of history?
