Let’s get right into today’s Daily Stoic passage: **Seeing Our Addictions**

> “We must give up many things to which we are addicted, considering them to be good. Otherwise, courage will vanish... Greatness of soul will be lost...” — Seneca, Moral Letters, 74.12, B-13

The commentary explores how small compulsions chip away at our freedom. Things that seem harmless — a cup of coffee, a cigarette, scrolling Instagram — are repetitive actions that drag us away from our purpose.

The passage invites us to take stock: what am I addicted to, and why? Are these actions getting me closer to the life I want? Or am I just numbing, coping, reacting?

Personally, I drink 3–5 cups of coffee a day. I say I can’t start without one — that’s something I’m going to work on. Since going to Israel recently, I also picked up smoking cigarettes again. Not as much as before, but even one a day is too many. It’s counterproductive. It’s not aligned with who I want to be.

But I’m not beating myself up. I’m taking stock. I’m staying proud of my progress — waking up early, walking, showing up for myself. And now I’m just looking at the next layer. The next refinements.

Take a moment today to do the same. Where are you strong? Where are you slipping? Don’t shame yourself. Just take inventory.

I love you all. Have a wonderful day. Let’s keep it moving, one breath, one walk, one decision at a time.
