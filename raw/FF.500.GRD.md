**Forever Flowers – Seasonal Collection Grid Layout**

**Design Logic:**
Inspired by Restoration Hardware and Habitat, this grid prioritizes minimalism, premium tone, and seamless conversion. Every element on the grid must earn its place—nothing decorative, everything intentional.

**Grid Elements per Item:**
1. **Hero Image:** Full-bleed, edge-to-edge. Crisp, matte aesthetic. Real bouquet, styled in natural light.
2. **Name:** One-line, Hebrew and English. Timeless naming structure. E.g., "לחישת השדה – Whisper of the Field."
3. **Poetic Tagline:** One short phrase (5–8 words max) to spark desire. Written in RH/Habitat tone. Example: "Preserved sunflowers from a vanished summer."
4. **Price:** Always visible. No hover, no reveal. Large enough to be noticed, subtle enough not to dominate.
5. **Action Button:** Primary CTA is either "Add to Bag" (if customization is minimal) or "View" (if bouquet includes multiple options).

**Grid Behavior:**
- Static layout, no carousels, no animation unless subtly tied to hover.
- RTL and LTR mirrored versions for Hebrew/English fluidity.
- Seasonal collections stacked by recency. Archived collections below with muted visual tone (e.g., grayscale, desaturated).
- Signature collection fixed at bottom or in separate floating section to signal permanence.

**Copy Tone:**
- Every word must justify the price.
- Zero marketing clichés. Speak like a designer, not a florist.
- Nothing speaks louder than restraint.

**Mobile Optimization:**
- Two-column grid max.
- CTA always visible beneath image, not floating.
- Prioritize scannability—no need to scroll twice to understand value.

This layout reflects Forever Flowers’ shift into a premium, editorial-style brand rooted in beauty, scarcity, and quiet confidence.
