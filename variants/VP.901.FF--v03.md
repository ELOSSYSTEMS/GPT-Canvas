## 🏡 Integrated Aesthetic Summary (Across Homes and Apartments)

The Israeli luxury visual language bifurcates into two cultural modes:

- **Aspirational Detached Homes:** architectural, serene, resort-like
- **Relatable High-End Apartments:** pragmatic minimalism, compact elegance

Together, they form the **visual tension of Israeli taste**: the dream of spatial expanse within the bounds of dense living.

### Updated Observations:

#### Private Villas (Moshavim / Savyon / Raanana):
- Sun-treated materials (microcement, stone, oak)
- Negative space is revered
- Light flows horizontally, often through courtyards
- Vignettes dominate over full-room visibility

#### High-End Apartments (Tel Aviv / Central Towers):
- **Compressed minimalism**: clean vertical lines, built-ins, sliding storage
- Light is **contained**, often artificial-white
- Wall art is present, but subdued
- Natural elements introduced in **controlled touches**: one plant, one bowl, one bouquet
- Spatial luxury is visual, not dimensional

## 🌌 Light Logic Refinement:

### Villa Context:
- Natural, warm, side-cast
- Photographic softness

### Apartment Context:
- Whitened directional LED or soft overcast window light
- **Diffuse bounce lighting** is key (no direct beams)

---

## 🎡 Placement Logic Across Contexts

### Universal Rules:
- One bouquet per space
- Always framed by one heavier neutral (e.g., bowl, lamp, ceramic sculpture)
- No more than 3 texture types in frame (e.g., linen, concrete, flower)

### Apartment-Specific:
- Console table in hallway
- Corner of kitchen island (with distance from clutter)
- Floating shelf / niche zone
- Bathroom corner (smaller arrangements)
