## 🏡 Integrated Aesthetic Summary (Based on 6 High-End Israeli Residences)

The Israeli luxury interior archetype is less about opulence and more about **grounded sophistication**. It favors **clean geometry, tactile contrast, and strategic stillness.** The homes studied reflect a consistent fingerprint:

- ■ Natural light is treated as a material.
- ■ Tone is warm-neutral, not sterile.
- ■ Materials honor **age and imperfection** (brushed metals, rough linens, raw concrete).
- ■ Minimalism is used as a **frame**, not a constraint.
- ■ There is often a **spatial pause** in every room: negative space that exudes calm.

The Forever Florals "Soft Structure" identity integrates seamlessly when treated as a **living sculptural object** in these environments.

## 🌌 Lighting Environment
**Direction:** Indirect, almost always side-lit
**Time of Day:** Late morning to golden hour
**Texture:** Softened shadows, no overheads

**Lighting Strategy for Prompts:**
> "Natural light entering from the side through oversized horizontal windows or sliding glass, illuminating a preserved bouquet resting in a matte ceramic vase. Shadows fall subtly against plaster or oak."

## 🎡 Spatial Composition Rules (based on observed layouts)

### A. Placement Rules for Bouquets:
- On a single large central surface (dining table, kitchen island, entry console)
- Never cluttered, often alone or flanked by one heavy neutral (e.g. a stone bowl or paper lantern)

### B. Object Pairing:
- Match with matte surfaces only
- Complement with high-tactile objects (book cloth, ceramic, aged metal)

### C. Environmental Context:
- Large open plan, light-washed
- Background should have a compositional **anchor**: a wall niche, a shelf with one visible book, a sculptural chair
